**Summary of the work:**

This manuscript presents a comprehensive unified field theory framework that addresses the long-standing challenge of reconciling quantum mechanics with general relativity. Our approach is distinguished from existing theories by three key features:

1. **Mathematical Rigor**: We establish a rigorous mathematical foundation based on fractal Clifford algebras and spectral dimension theory. The core theorems—including the decomposition of the covering map kernel ker(ρ^τ) ≅ Z₂⁵ and its correspondence to the Standard Model gauge group U(1)×SU(2)×SU(3)—are proven from first principles without heuristic arguments.

2. **Experimental Accessibility**: The theory makes concrete, falsifiable predictions: six gravitational wave polarization modes (versus two in general relativity), characteristic corrections to the CMB power spectrum (n_s ≈ 0.965 ± 0.02, r ≈ 0.01–0.05), and modified atomic energy levels. These predictions are within reach of current and near-future experiments including LIGO/Virgo, CMB-S4, and next-generation atomic clocks.

3. **Resolution of Scale Discrepancy**: Through a nonlinear self-interaction mechanism, the theory naturally reconciles the stringent atomic physics constraints (τ < 10⁻¹⁴) with cosmological predictions (τ ~ 0.01) without fine-tuning.

**Novelty and Significance:**

Unlike string theory, which suffers from the landscape problem and lacks clear experimental predictions, or loop quantum gravity, which does not incorporate the Standard Model gauge structure, our framework provides a geometric derivation of all four fundamental interactions from a unified spacetime structure. The theory is falsifiable: if next-generation gravitational wave detectors (Cosmic Explorer, Einstein Telescope, ~2035) fail to detect the predicted additional polarization modes at the sensitivity level corresponding to τ ~ 0.03, the core framework would be experimentally excluded.
