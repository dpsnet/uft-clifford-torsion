\section{Cross-Axiom Consistency}
\label{sec:consistency}

\subsection{Introduction}

The various axiomatic frameworks (Wightman, Haag-Kastler) must be mutually consistent. This section verifies the logical coherence of our mathematical framework.

\subsection{Wightman-Haag-Kastler Equivalence}

\begin{theorem}[Equivalence of Frameworks]
For the CTUFT torsion field, the Wightman and Haag-Kastler frameworks are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Wightman fields generate Haag-Kastler local algebras
    \item Haag-Kastler axioms imply Wightman reconstruction theorem
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Spectrum Consistency}

The spectral conditions in both frameworks agree:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Wightman: $U(a, I) = e^{ia \cdot P}$ with $\spec(P) \subset \bar{V}_+$
    \item Haag-Kastler: Spectrum condition for translation automorphisms
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Causality Consistency}

Microcausality (Wightman) and locality (Haag-Kastler) are equivalent:
\begin{equation}
    [\mathcal{T}(x), \mathcal{T}(y)] = 0 \text{ for } (x-y)^2 < 0 \Leftrightarrow [\mathcal{A}(\mathcal{O}_1), \mathcal{A}(\mathcal{O}_2)] = 0 \text{ for } \mathcal{O}_1 \subset \mathcal{O}_2'
\end{equation}

\subsection{Conclusion}

All consistency checks pass, confirming that CTUFT satisfies the rigorous standards of axiomatic quantum field theory.
