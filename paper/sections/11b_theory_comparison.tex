\subsubsection{Mathematical Equivalence?}

Despite differences, UFT and string theory may be mathematically equivalent descriptions:

\begin{conjecture}[UFT-String Duality]
UFT in the $\alpha' \to 0$ limit corresponds to the low-energy effective field theory of Type IIA/IIB string theory compactified on a Calabi-Yau manifold with torsion flux.
\end{conjecture}

Evidence:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Both use $Cl(1,9)$ Clifford algebra
    \item Both predict 6 extra dimensions
    \item Both achieve gauge coupling unification
    \item UFT's fractal structure $\leftrightarrow$ string worldsheet geometry
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Supersymmetry (SUSY) Comparison}

\subsubsection{The MSSM Problem}

The Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM) was the leading candidate for physics beyond the Standard Model, but:
\begin{itemize}
    \item No SUSY particles observed at LHC (up to $\sim$ 2 TeV)
    \item $124+$ free parameters in full MSSM
    \item No naturalness solution (little hierarchy problem)
    \item Proton decay constraints violated in simple models
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Supersymmetry in UFT?}

UFT does not require SUSY, but admits it as an extension:

\begin{proposition}[SUSY as Optional Structure]
Supersymmetry can be incorporated into UFT by extending the Clifford algebra to include Grassmann variables:
\begin{equation}
    Cl(1,9) \rightarrow Cl(1,9) \otimes \Lambda(\theta, \bar{\theta})
\end{equation}
where $\Lambda$ is the Grassmann algebra. However, this is not required for consistency.
\end{proposition}
