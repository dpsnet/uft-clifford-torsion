\section{Entanglement Geometry}
\label{sec:entanglement}

Entanglement geometry explores the geometric interpretation of quantum entanglement within the CTUFT framework.

\subsection{Entanglement as Geometric Connection}

In CTUFT, quantum entanglement between two particles is understood as a geometric connection through the internal space.

\begin{insight}[Entanglement = Internal Space Bridge]
Entangled particles share a common internal space coordinate, creating a "bridge" that persists even when the particles are spatially separated in the reciprocal (4D) space.
\end{insight}

\subsection{Entanglement Entropy}

The entanglement entropy between two subsystems $A$ and $B$ is given by:
\begin{equation}
S_A = -\text{Tr}(\rho_A \ln \rho_A)
\end{equation}

where $\rho_A$ is the reduced density matrix of subsystem $A$.

\subsection{Geometric Entanglement Formula}

CTUFT provides a geometric formula for entanglement entropy:
\begin{equation}
S_A = \frac{\text{Area}(\gamma_A)}{4G_{\text{eff}}}
\end{equation}

where $\gamma_A$ is the minimal surface in the internal space separating region $A$ from its complement.
