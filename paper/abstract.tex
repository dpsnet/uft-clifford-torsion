\begin{abstract}

We present a Unified Field Theory (UFT) based on the principle that 4D reciprocal space and 10D internal space are dual to each other—energy flowing inward in reciprocal space corresponds to outward expansion in internal space. This ``fixed 4D topology--dynamic spectral dimension'' framework resolves fundamental puzzles in quantum gravity while making precise, testable predictions.

The theory is built on Clifford algebra $Cl(1,9)$, with the single parameter $\tau_0 = 0.005$ (derived from first principles via information theory and representation theory) determining all physical predictions. The torsion field mediates the flow between spaces, with its strength $\tau_0$ naturally small due to the 4D/10D projection geometry.

Our key results include: (1) All twelve fermion masses predicted from $\tau_0$ with sub-percent accuracy ($\chi^2 = 2.13$, $p = 0.998$); (2) CKM matrix elements with 1.9\% average deviation; (3) Gauge coupling unification at $E_c = 2\times 10^{21}$ GeV; (4) Dark energy density $\Lambda = 3\tau_0^2/2\kappa^2$ matching observations within 60\%; (5) Dark matter as primordial black holes ($M \sim 10^{-12} M_\odot$) from torsion fluctuations; (6) Black hole information preservation via flow to internal space; (7) Resolution of the cosmological constant problem without fine-tuning; (8) Geometric origin of the strong CP problem and hierarchy problem.

Unlike the Standard Model's 19+ free parameters or supersymmetry's 124+ parameters, UFT requires \textbf{zero free parameters}—all quantities emerge from geometry. The theory makes falsifiable predictions: scalar gravitational wave polarizations at 0.5\% amplitude, no proton decay below $10^{35}$ years, specific corrections to atomic clock redshifts, and asteroid-mass primordial black holes as dark matter.

\end{abstract}
